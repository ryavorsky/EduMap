LEGAL PROTECTION OF CHILDHOOD IN THE RUSSIAN FEDERATION
N. Yu. Kiryushina, L. B. Morozova
Abstract. The article analyzes the qualitative state of child protection system prevailing in Russia.
It concludes that the national legislation contains a wide range of guaranteed rights of minors in accordance
with the international standards. In this case the state mechanisms to protect and restore the
rights of children suffer defects. Appeal to the law on basics of prevention of child neglect and juvenile
delinquency indicates, first, the intention of the legislator to provide legal protection to all children and
families at risk, and secondly, the absence of such mechanisms. It is considered that there is a need to
revise the conceptual framework of the system for the protection of children in socially dangerous situation
towards determining the subjects of authority and responsibility. Of central importance is the
formation of the legal framework of interagency cooperation.
Keywords: children’s rights, protection of children’s rights, family law, juvenile, child neglect, crime,
prevention system, inter-agency cooperation.

Abstract. The article considers current issues related to proof in civil cases arising from labor relations.

LEGAL FRAMEWORK OF OUTSOURCING IN EDUCATION
O. V. Puzatkin
Abstract. The article discusses the legal framework of use of outsourcing in the field of education
and presents the examples of activities that can be delegated to external execution. The economic essence
of outsourcing is to construct a system of partnerships between organizations which comply with
committing certain activities on the basis of concluded long-term agreements. As an economic concept,
the term “outsourcing” is not represented in the Labor Code, which causes some difficulties in using it
in educational institutions. The article explains that the outsourcing relationship between the educational
organization and the outsourcer may be governed by civil law contracts for the provision of paid
services, and between the organization and the outsourcer staff that they provide for the performance
of work by the employment contract. The article also reveals the main points that need to be taken into
account when concluding the contract of outsourcing in order to minimize possible risks in the transmission
of ancillary activities to external execution.
Keywords: outsourcing, outsourcer, outsourcing services, outsourcing agreement, contract of compensated
rendering of services, outsourcing risks.

AND PRACTICE OF SONATAL-PEDAGOGY)
M. L. Lazarev
Abstract. The article sets out the concept of the formation of the personality of a child before and after
birth, which developed a system of harmonization of child development - SONATAL-pedagogy. The article
also describes the results of the population-based studies on the application of the method „Sonatal” on a
large group of pregnant women and their children in Naberezhnye Chelny from 1995 to 2015. These studies
suggest that the method „Sonatal” contributes to the harmonization of the development of cognitive and
somatic spheres of child and smoothing the age crises at 3 and 7 year-olds, increases the level of parental
competence and responsibility in the area of recovery and development of a child. In general, the results
provide a basis for the inclusion of the prenatal period of ontogenesis of children in the age range of the
domestic activities of psychology, pedagogy and pediatrics – „from the moment of conception till 18 years
of age”, with the introduction of corresponding amendments to the relevant legislation documents that will
help in solving a number of important public issues such as demography, health status of children, the formation
of responsible parents, and give impetus to the creation of a national and international movement
for the upbringing healthy and harmonious personality of the child before and after birth.
Keywords: prenatal education, the „Sonatal” method, the rights of a prenatal child, cognosomic psychology,
prenatal childhood.
